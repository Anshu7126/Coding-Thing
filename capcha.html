What is CAPTCHA

CAPTCHA stands for “Completely Automated Public Turing test to tell Computers and Humans Apart” CAPTCHAs are tools you can use to differentiate between real users and automated users, such as bots.

How does it work?

It asks the users to interpret a distorted text that a user then has to submit via a form field which supposedly a bot cannot identify. This limitation makes it unlikely that bots will correctly guess the right combination


Why do we need it?

There are innumerous bots on the internet. the goal is to block all of these bots to reduce the bot activity on websites.


How to create a CAPTCHA?

Google provides a service called as “reCAPTCHA”


  • Register your website on reCAPTCHA.
  • Add the reCAPTCHA script to your website.
  • Place the CAPTCHA on your site.
Who uses CAPTCHA?

  • One of the most common application is verifying online polls.
  • Registration forms on websites use CAPTCHA to prevent fake registrations.
  • Ticket brokers use CAPTCHA to prevent from creating unnecessary traffic on the server.
Drawbacks of Using CAPTCHA

  • Disruptive and frustrating for users.
  • May be difficult to understand or use for some audiences.
  • Some CAPTCHA types do not support all browsers.
CAPTCHA Types: Examples

  • Text-based CAPTCHAs.
  • CAPTCHA Image.
  • Audio CAPTCHA.
  • Math or Word Problems.
  • Social Media Sign In.
  • No CAPTCHA ReCAPTCHA.
